Introduction

Blind Crossword 3D is a computer program that enables blind and partially sighted crossword solvers to tackle three dimensional crossword puzzles.

Three dimensional crossword puzzles can be thought of as layers of regular crosswords. TODO

The program is fully voiced - you should receive voiced feedback for any action you take in the program. At any point while using the program you can hit M to have the last voiced message read back to you.

Quick Start Instructions

Players can hit H to open this help manual at any time with the program in focus. For a separate, more detailed quick start tutorial, follow the link here TODO. For a fast and brief introduction, follow the instructions below:

  1. Make sure the program is open.
  2. Hit L to open the list of crossword files. Use the arrow keys to move through the list of files. Press enter to load the selected crossword.
  3. You can now use the arrow keys to move through the table of clues. Details about each clue will be read out. Press enter to open a text box for entering your answer.
  4. Type a guess in and press enter to submit your answer. Was your answer accepted? Can you validate your answer by hitting V?
  5. Look at the controls list here and explore!

Controls List

This is a reference list of controls, listed in rough order of importance, with brief descriptions:

  • Q - quit the program
  • L - open a list of crossword
  • S - saves the current crossword
  • H - opens the help document in your web browser
  • K - tells you a current score for your crossword if published solutions are contained within the crossword file
  • P - tells you information about the crossword, including the title and authors
  • F - filters entries. Tap this multiple times to cycle through the available filters
  • J - tap this multiple times to cycle through the available speech modes
  • X - tells you the theme phrase that goes with this crossword, if any
  • Z - cuts the voice short
  • N - toggles the idle reminder that reminds you that the application is still running and how to open this help page
  • O - reads a single word of a clue and advances a word, so that the next time you press it, the next word in the clue will be spoken
  • I - reads a single word of a clue but does not advance a word
  • 0 - increases the speech rate of the voice
  • 9 - decreases the speech rate of the voice
  • Return/Enter - used for confirming choices and dialog submissions
  • V - validates a guess. Removes incorrect letters from the currently selected guess if published solutions are contained within the crossword file
  • Backspace - erases the current selected guess. Note that erased letters also appear from adjoining items
  • W - tells you the day number for the selected item
  • E - tells you the entry, the numbers and associated directions, of the selected item
  • R - tells you the current guess of the selected item
  • T - tells you the clue for the selected item
  • Y - tells you the solution structure for the selected item
  • 1 - sorts the items by day number
  • 2
  • 3 - sorts items by alphabetical order of clue
  • 4

Loading Crosswords

With the program running, you can hit L to open the list of crossword files. Use the up and down arrow keys to move through the list of files - the voice will read the name of the current file. Hit Return or Enter to load the file. Once the file is loaded, you can move onto immediately solving it. Alternatively, hit Escape to close the loading dialog, and continue to use the program.

You can hit L at any time to re-open the list of crossword files. Remember that if you want to save your progress on an existing open crossword, you must do so before loading a new crossword.

Saving Crosswords

Hit S to save the currently open crossword. The crossword file is automatically saved with the title of the open crossword. If a crossword file of the same name already exists, then the crossword is saved using the crossword the title followed by an underscore followed by a number.

Navigating the Clues Table

You can use the arrow keys to navigate the clues table. The table has the following columns:

  • Date - CalendarPuzzles crosswords have a clue for each day, and so are numbered by date. Some puzzles even have solutions that are in alphabetical order of the date.
  • Entry Name - Each clue entry name consists of the numbers and directions that the clue takes. Some clues snake around the grids, and so can take multiple numbers and directions!
  • Guess - Your guess at the answer to the clue.
  • Clue - The clue that you must solve.
  • Solution Structure - The structure of the solution - the length of words in the answer, and the puncutation that comes in between those words.

The voice will read out details of clues as you go navigate through the table. You can hit the W, E, R, T and Y to read different parts of the clue.

It is possible to sort clues. Tapping numbers 1, 2, 3 and 4 will sort clues by the day they appear on, their word length, and the alphabetical order of the clues.

It is possible to filter clues. Tapping F repeatedly will filter clues by the direction, whether they have been started (i.e. contain letters), or whether they are unstarted.

Entering Answers

Whilst navigating the clues list, you can hit Return or Enter to open a dialog for entering your answers.

Type your guess in. If you made a mistake, then the program will tell you the problem. You can hit Return or Enter and enter another guess.

You can erase or delete a guess by hitting backspace.

Validating Answers

If the crossword you are solving contains a set of answers, you may validate your clues by selecting the desired clue and pressing V. If there are any incorrect letters in the guess, they will be removed from the guess.

If a full set of answers are available to the program, then you can get an overall grade/mark for your guesses by pressing K.

Navigating the Graphical Grid View

Currently blind users cannot navigate the graphical grid. However, users with some sight can view the grid by checking the box in the Options Menu, or by pressing TODO.

The grid can be zoomed by scrolling your mouse wheel over the grid. If you do not have a scroll wheel, tapping the TODO keys can achieve the same effect.

You can move around the graphical grid view by left clicking and dragging your mouse across the view. You can also drag grids around by clicking on them and moving your mouse.

Printing Crossword Answers

To print your answers, press F. This will bring up a dialog which you must work with using your screen reader, in order to confirm that you wish to print. The printed document will include a list containing your answers and the Calendar Puzzles mailing address.

Back to Contents

Emailing Crossword Answers

To email your answers, press G. This will open up an email in your default mail program. It will be addressed to Calendar Puzzles and will contain a list that includes your answers.

Obtaining Crosswords

You can download completed crosswords and new crosswords from the CalendarPuzzles website. The website is accessible here:
http://www.calendarpuzzles.co.uk/

Once you have downloaded a crossword file, you must move it into the Crosswords folder inside the Blind Crossword 3D folder. Once you have done this, you can open the load crossword dialog (reopen it if it was open to begin with) in the program - you should find that your downloaded crossword file appears in the list.

Known Issues

Issue #1 - Some words are pronounced incorrectly by the voice. This depends on the SAPI voice that is being used.

Solution #1 - You can press Key J to cycle through different speech modes. You can also press Keys I and O to read a clue you are having difficulty word by word.
